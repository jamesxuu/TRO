Probabilistic Feedback Control Enhances Multi-Legged Locomotion

Abstract

Achieving robust legged locomotion on complex terrains poses challenges due to uncertainty in robot-environment interactions. We propose a probabilistic feedback control strategy for multi-legged robots that improves locomotion speed and stability across lab and outdoor rough terrain. We use vertical body undulation as a control parameter, guided by binary foot-ground contact sensors, to mitigate environmental disturbance. Experiments on 8- and 12-legged robots show 30â€“60% improvement in speed with reduced variance.
